# Social Content Pillars

## Context

Use this skill when the user needs a monthly content calendar, content strategy, or post templates for social media across one or more brands/platforms. This skill covers content pillar definition, platform-specific formatting, caption writing, and scheduling workflows.

**Trigger phrases:** "content calendar," "social media strategy," "content pillars," "monthly posts," "social media templates," "content plan for [brand]."

---

## Instructions

### Step 1: Define Brand Voice

1. **For each brand, document:**
   - Brand name and industry
   - Target audience (age range, interests, platform preferences)
   - Voice traits: choose 3 from (professional, casual, witty, educational, inspiring, provocative, empathetic, authoritative)
   - Topics to AVOID (competitor mentions, politics, sensitive subjects)
   - Visual style: color palette, font style, image treatment (photos, illustrations, screenshots)

2. **Create a brand voice reference card:**
   ```
   [Brand] sounds like [celebrity/public figure] talking about [topic].
   We always: [2 tone rules]
   We never: [2 tone rules]
   Example caption: "Your best writing sample here."
   ```

### Step 2: Set Content Pillars

3. **Define 3-5 content pillars** per brand. Each pillar must have:
   - Pillar name (2-3 words)
   - Description (one sentence)
   - Percentage of monthly posts allocated (must sum to 100%)
   - 3-5 example topic ideas

   Example pillars:
   ```
   1. Educational Tips (40%) - Actionable how-to content
   2. Industry Insights (25%) - News, trends, data
   3. Behind the Scenes (20%) - Team, process, culture
   4. Social Proof (15%) - Testimonials, results, case studies
   ```

4. **Map pillars to platforms** based on audience behavior:
   - Instagram: visual-first pillars (behind-the-scenes, carousels, Reels)
   - Twitter/X: timely insights, threads, hot takes
   - LinkedIn: long-form, professional insights, company culture
   - TikTok: trending formats, tutorials, personality-driven content
   - YouTube: deep dives, tutorials, interviews

### Step 3: Generate Monthly Calendar

5. **Create a 30-day calendar** structured as:
   ```
   | Date | Day     | Platform  | Pillar       | Format          | Topic/Idea              | Status |
   |------|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------------|--------|
   | 1    | Monday  | LinkedIn  | Educational  | Text + Image    | 5 mistakes in [topic]   | Draft  |
   | 1    | Monday  | Instagram | Behind Scenes| Carousel        | Team workspace tour     | Draft  |
   | 2    | Tuesday | Twitter   | Industry     | Thread          | My take on [news]       | Draft  |
   ```

6. **Posting frequency guidelines:**
   - Instagram: 4-5 posts/week + 3-5 Stories/day
   - Twitter/X: 1-3 posts/day
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 posts/week
   - TikTok: 3-5 posts/week
   - YouTube: 1-2 videos/week

7. **Batch similar content.** Dedicate specific days to pillars:
   - Monday: Educational (start the week with value)
   - Wednesday: Industry insights (mid-week engagement)
   - Friday: Behind-the-scenes (end-of-week personality)

### Step 4: Create Post Templates

8. **Caption template per pillar:**
   ```
   EDUCATIONAL:
   Hook (1 line, question or bold statement)
   Context (1-2 sentences)
   Actionable tip(s) (numbered list, 3-5 items)
   CTA ("Save this for later" / "Follow for more")

   INDUSTRY INSIGHT:
   Hook (news reference or contrarian take)
   Your perspective (2-3 sentences)
   Implication (what this means for the audience)
   Question (to drive comments)

   SOCIAL PROOF:
   Result headline (specific numbers)
   Brief story (who, what, outcome)
   Lesson learned (1-2 sentences)
   CTA ("Want similar results? Link in bio")
   ```

9. **Hashtag strategy per platform:**
   - Instagram: 20-30 hashtags (mix of niche #100-999k, medium #10k-100k, broad #1M+)
   - Twitter/X: 1-3 hashtags maximum
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 hashtags, industry-specific
   - TikTok: 3-5 hashtags (include trending sounds/hashtags when relevant)

10. **Engagement hooks library** (rotate these, never repeat within 2 weeks):
    - "Most people get [topic] wrong. Here's what actually works:"
    - "I spent [time] learning [topic]. Here's the summary:"
    - "Unpopular opinion: [contrarian take about industry topic]"
    - "The #1 thing I wish I knew about [topic] before starting:"
    - "[Number] [resources/tips/tools] that changed how I approach [topic]"

### Step 5: Repurposing & Scheduling

11. **Repurposing matrix** (create once, publish everywhere):
    ```
    Source: LinkedIn long-form post
      → Twitter: Extract key points as a thread
      → Instagram: Turn into a carousel (1 point per slide)
      → TikTok: Script as a talking-head video
      → YouTube: Expand into a 5-10 minute deep dive

    Source: Instagram Reel
      → TikTok: Cross-post (adjust caption for TikTok audience)
      → YouTube Shorts: Cross-post
      → Twitter: Post as a video tweet with key takeaway
    ```

12. **Scheduling workflow:**
    - Use a scheduling tool (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite, or native platform tools)
    - Batch-create content in weekly blocks (Sundays or Mondays)
    - Schedule 1-2 weeks in advance
    - Leave 20% of slots open for reactive/trending content

---

## Constraints

- NEVER post the exact same caption across platforms. Adapt tone and length per platform.
- NEVER use more than 2 emojis per line in any caption.
- NEVER buy followers, engagement, or use engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree").
- Content pillars MUST be defined before any calendar generation. No calendar without pillars.
- Each post MUST have a clear purpose (educate, engage, convert, or build awareness).
- Brand voice MUST remain consistent across all platforms. No platform-specific personas.
- Always include a CTA in every post (even if it's just "save this" or "share with someone who needs this").
- Respect platform-specific best practices: no links in Instagram captions (use bio/Stories), no external links in LinkedIn post body.

## Examples

### Example 1: SaaS Startup (B2B)

```
Brand: CloudMetrics (analytics platform)
Voice: Authoritative + Educational + Witty
Audience: CTOs and engineering managers (30-50)

Pillars:
  1. Data Insights (40%) - Industry benchmarks, trend analysis
  2. Product Tips (30%) - How to use CloudMetrics features
  3. Engineering Culture (20%) - Remote work, team practices
  4. Customer Stories (10%) - Case studies, ROI metrics

Weekly Calendar (LinkedIn + Twitter focus):
  Mon: Data Insight post (LinkedIn article, Twitter thread)
  Tue: Product Tip (LinkedIn carousel, Twitter tip)
  Thu: Engineering Culture (LinkedIn text post, Twitter hot take)
  Fri: Curated roundup (5 links, LinkedIn + Twitter)
```

### Example 2: Personal Brand (Creator)

```
Brand: @designbytes (UX designer, 25K followers)
Voice: Casual + Educational + Inspiring
Audience: Junior designers and product managers (22-35)

Pillars:
  1. Design Tutorials (45%) - UI/UX how-tos, Figma tips
  2. Career Advice (25%) - Portfolio reviews, job tips
  3. Design Critiques (20%) - Breakdowns of real products
  4. Personal Journey (10%) - Freelance life, lessons learned

Repurposing:
  Figma tutorial recording → YouTube video → Instagram Reel (60s cut) →
  Twitter thread (5 key steps) → LinkedIn carousel (same 5 steps)
```
